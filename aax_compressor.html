Dynamic Range Compressor

A Dynamic Range Compressor limits or extends the dynamic gain range of the audio signal by increasing or decreasing the loudness of softer signal levels over time. The software library implements Upward compression.

Low volume periods will be amplified by this filter and high volume periods will not be unaltered.

The Compressor is supported by the Mixer and Audio-Frames.
Compressor parameters
The filter uses up to two filter slots.
AAX_COMPRESSOR
STEREO

Slot 0
p0
AAX_ATTACK_RATE
The time it takes to respond to an increase in volume.
Ranges between 0.001 (1 ms) and 0.25 (250 ms).
p1
AAX_RELEASE_RATE
The time it takes to respond to a decrease in volume.
Ranges between 0.001 (1 ms) and 10.0 (seconds).
p2
AAX_COMPRESSION_RATIO
Defines the gain level of the altered signal.
Between 0.0 and 1.0
  • 0.0 means no change (a ratio of 1:1)
  • 1.0 means an infinite amplification ratio (∞:1)
p3
AAX_THRESHOLD
Signal levels that are below the threshold level will get amplified.
Signal levels that are larger than the threshold level will pass unaltered.

Slot 1
p0
 
Unused.
p1
AAX_GATE_PERIOD
Defines the time it takes for the noise gate to respond to a volume change.
Ranges between 0.001 (1 ms) and 10.0 (seconds), defaults to 0.25 (250 ms)
p2
 
Unused.
p3
AAX_GATE_THRESHOLD
Signal levels that are below the gate threshold will clipped to 0 by the filter.
This is useful to eliminate static noise during quiet periods in the audio signal.
Between 0.0 and 1.0, defaults to 0.0
Compressor State options
Specify an envelope following type to enable the frequency filter or false to disable it.
AAX_COMPRESSOR
AAX_ENVELOPE_FOLLOW
AAX_INVERSE_ENVELOPE_FOLLOW

All waveforms may be combined with AAX_ENVELOPE_FOLLOW_LOG or AAX_INVERSE_ENVELOPE_FOLLOW_LOG to create exponential behaviour, e.g. AAX_ENVELOPE_FOLLOW|AAX_ENVELOPE_FOLLOW_LOG
